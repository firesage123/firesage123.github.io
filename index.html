About Me

Hello! My name is James He and I am currently a junior at Northwestern University double majoring in Computer Science and Applied Mathematics as well as pursuing a minor in History.

I am an aspiring computer scientist looking to get some real-world experience, both to help me expand my learning as well as to hopefully make a difference in the world.

One of the main areas of focus for my undergraduate degree and something that I am extremely interested in is theoretical computer science, particularly computational theory and algorithmic design. I have worked as a teaching assistant for Professors Ming-Yang Kao and Aninyda De, both of whom are members of Northwestern's CS Theory group and hope to get more opportunities in this area of research.

In my free time, some things I enjoy doing include football, reading history books, playing Starcraft II, and visiting old thrift shops.

Thor

This project was created for my high school AP Computer Science class. Thor is a platform mini-game written in Java using the GridWorld API. The player controls a single character (Thor) and the objective of the game is to kill all the aliens that spawn on the map. Java was the very first programming language I learned and Thor was my first ever project.
